Legendary Synthesizers in Pop Culture

Moog Minimoog

Introduced in 1970, the Minimoog revolutionized popular music with its portable design and iconic sound. It became a staple in progressive rock, funk, and electronic music.

Heard in:
• "Superstition" by Stevie Wonder
• "Lucky Man" by Emerson, Lake & Palmer
• "Chameleon" by Herbie Hancock

Roland TR-808

Released in 1980, the TR-808 drum machine became the backbone of hip-hop, electronic, and pop music. Its unique, punchy sounds defined entire genres.

Heard in:
• "Planet Rock" by Afrika Bambaataa
• "Sexual Healing" by Marvin Gaye
• "Oh Yeah" by Yello

Yamaha DX7

Launched in 1983, the DX7 introduced digital synthesis and became the defining sound of 1980s pop and new wave music. Its electric piano and bell-like tones were ubiquitous.

Heard in:
• "Take On Me" by A-ha
• "What's Love Got to Do with It" by Tina Turner
• "Axel F" by Harold Faltermeyer

Sequential Circuits Prophet-5

Released in 1978, the Prophet-5 was the first fully programmable polyphonic synthesizer. It became a must-have for professional musicians across multiple genres.

Heard in:
• "Africa" by Toto
• Many Michael Jackson and Prince tracks
• "Jump" by Van Halen

Korg MS-20

Introduced in 1978, the MS-20 was known for its raw, aggressive sound and unique semi-modular design. It became a favorite among experimental and electronic musicians.

Heard in:
• Works by Portishead
• Aphex Twin recordings
• Nine Inch Nails productions

ARP 2600

Launched in 1971, the ARP 2600 was a groundbreaking semi-modular synthesizer used in film scores, progressive rock, and electronic music. Its versatile sound shaped multiple genres.

Heard in:
• Star Wars sound effects
• Kraftwerk recordings
• Many early electronic film scores

Oberheim OB-Xa

Released in 1981, the OB-Xa was a powerful polyphonic synthesizer that defined the lush, thick sounds of 1980s pop and new wave music.

Heard in:
• "Blue Monday" by New Order
• Many synth-pop and new wave tracks
• Prince's early recordings
